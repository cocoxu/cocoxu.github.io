Best Dressed NLP Researchers (2016 ~ 2017)

Editor's Notes
The competition for the best dressed list is fierce. The winners are picked from a 2-year period including the record-breaking ACL 2017 with a 1800+ crowd. Good news is that there is only one reviewer (me) other than three reviewers to convince that "your RNN is better than others RNNs". The criteria are:
  • Appropriateness: Does this outfit fit the conference?
  • Originality: Does this outfit show innovative ideas? Or merely the same as what wore before or wore by others?
  • Soundness: Is the outfit well-chosen to support the author's personality?
The growth of NLP conferences and research community provided a lot of more eye candies and excitements. At the same time, conferences become less intimate and more intimidating. It becomes increasingly challenging to young students for their first conferences, so here are my two tips:
  • Stay out of comfort zone! Large conference is not meant to feel comfortable, so if you are doing things you feel extremely uncomfortable then you are likely doing the right thing. If you are students talk to renowned researchers just as an equal, if you are a Chinese student talk to non-Chinese researchers just as the same, etc.;
  • Stay late and hang out! Everyone is just more relaxed and more friendly over dinner, and especially after dinner over drinks (see the infographic Keenan Crane made).

The 2015 best dressed list is here and 2014 is here. If you want to nominate someone, please email me at weixu@cse.ohio-state.edu. I have also received self-nominations :) and I am definitely taking them into accounts.

-- Wei Xu Sep 24, 2017
